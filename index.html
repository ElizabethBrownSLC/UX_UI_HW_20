Introduction

Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, people have been encouraged to social distance and avoid crowded areas. One problem that we suspect will arise is that people will need to start looking for vacation and travel ideas outside of major cities. Great travel attractions and destinations exist outside of major cities and tourist destinations, but it is often difficult for travelers to get information on ‘off the beaten path’ vacation spots. Travelers would benefit from a travel app where lesser-known attractions and destinations are featured and available to those looking to travel somewhere with no crowds. This app would allow users to browse new places by location, interests or their budget.

My Role: Research, Ideation, Design, Prototyping, Testing

The Team: Elizabeth Brown, Carly Mahoney

Problem Statement

All time visitation records are being broken at national parks. This is creating a need to encourage people to travel to lesser-visited destinations, guided experiences and off-season travel.

Reseaching Our User

One of the exercises we conducted to understand our user more was to interview a variety of people. These interviews provided us with data that we used to create an affinity diagram. We were able to see how users planned their vacations and activities and what technologies and features the user enjoyed in other applications.




After analyzing all the data we gathered from our research we were introduced to Sarah Smith. Sarah was our ideal user, our decision making from here on out would be centered on what would meet Sarah's wants and needs. Our main goal was to please Sarah and provide her with a tool that she would enjoy.




Ideating User Focused Features

We used the I Like... I Wish... What If... ideation method. Brainstorming like this helped us look at what our user wanted in the present, the near future, and the distant future. We then organized those ideas into a feature prioritization matrix in order to give Sarah features that would have the most impact.




Remotely - Go off the beaten path

Remotely helps you search and share lesser known travel opportunities. Create memorable experiences and enjoy unique activities. Have the peace of mind you are traveling safely. Let Remotely relieve the stress of planning your next adventure.

Getting Remotely Into the Hands of Testers and Making Iterations

We completed the prototyping and set up exercises to test the features. During testing we discovered the testers needed a more linear path to complete the task of planning a vacation. The first screen was a dashboard that allowed the user to explore the app but created a distraction from the task. We changed the app to place the user within the planning vacation step right after on boarding and then broke that screen into two. The first screen provided a fun interaction of choosing a travel persona and then they could set custom details about the vacation they wanted to take. This helped solve some confusion if the user had strayed away from the main task.










Creating User Focused Features

After the research, the ideation, and the prototyping we knew what features would make Remotely an enjoyable experience for Sarah. We knew Remotely had to offer a way for users to customize their trips. Once the user set their customization they could then select from featured destinations or plan their own trip. Users would be able to see maps, pictures, and ratings of destinations and activities. Finally they could invite and share their itineraries..
